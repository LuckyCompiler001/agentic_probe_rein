Iteration 4 changes (cooccur_delta_frobenius probe; threshold 0.15; lower is better)

Prior result (probe_result_3): tail_mean=0.2196 (FAIL). Big win vs. iter 2 (0.8009) so
iter 2's changes were kept (no revert). The curve now descends smoothly from 0.51 to
~0.20 but plateaus in the 0.20-0.25 band, oscillating (std 0.067, min 0.199 at
epoch 29). It never crosses the 0.15 threshold.

Diagnosis of the remaining gap:
The probe binarizes predictions at sigmoid>=0.5 before computing the 40x40 Pearson
correlation matrix. CelebA has extreme per-attribute imbalance: Bald ~2%, Mustache
~4%, Gray_Hair ~4%, Wearing_Hat ~5%, Double_Chin ~5%, Pale_Skin ~4%, 5_o_Clock_Shadow
~11%. Under unweighted BCEWithLogitsLoss the optimizer minimizes expected loss by
sending rare-class logits well below zero, so those attributes never fire at the
0.5 threshold. Their P_pred columns become constant (-> NaN -> 0 via nan_to_num),
destroying the real co-occurrences (Bald<->Male, Gray_Hair<->Old, Mustache<->Male,
etc.) that dominate P_train's off-diagonal structure. This, plus the late-epoch
oscillation, explains why tail_mean is stuck ~0.22 instead of <0.15.

None of the prior iterations tried class-imbalance weighting or an LR schedule.

Changes (all in lightningmodules/classification.py; one coherent intervention):

1. Add setup(stage) that reads the training label tensor (trainer.datamodule.train.attr,
   same access path the prober uses), computes pos_weight = N_neg / N_pos per
   attribute (clamped to <=15.0 to avoid destabilizing ultra-rare classes), registers
   it as a buffer (so Lightning moves it to the correct device), and replaces
   self.loss with nn.BCEWithLogitsLoss(pos_weight=...). This is the primary fix:
   rare attributes now receive meaningful gradient signal and can fire above 0.5
   at a realistic rate, restoring their rows/columns of P_pred.

2. Switch Adam -> AdamW(lr=self.lr, weight_decay=0.01). Standard ViT fine-tuning
   choice; the decoupled weight decay gently regularizes the backbone so the head
   does not shortcut-couple attributes it shouldn't.

3. Wrap the optimizer with CosineAnnealingLR(T_max=max_epochs, eta_min=lr*0.05),
   stepped per epoch. Iter 3's curve clearly oscillates in epochs 10-29 at the fixed
   5e-5 LR; a cosine decay to ~2.5e-6 by the last epochs should stabilize the tail
   5 epochs (the ones that determine the PASS/FAIL tail_mean).
