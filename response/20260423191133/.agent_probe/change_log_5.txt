Iter 4 made the metric MUCH worse (0.22 -> 0.49), so iter 4's interventions were
reverted. train.py was already identical to snapshot v3 (no revert needed there).
The regression was in lightningmodules/classification.py: iter 4 had bundled three
changes at once (pos_weight-weighted BCE with clamp=15, Adam -> AdamW with
weight_decay=0.01, CosineAnnealingLR with eta_min=lr*0.05). I reverted all three
so the module returns to the iter-3 baseline (plain Adam, unweighted
BCEWithLogitsLoss, no setup() override).

Post-mortem of iter 4: the most plausible culprit is the pos_weight (up to 15x).
The probe computes Pearson correlations of *binarized* predictions; pos_weight
pushes the logits for rare attributes so aggressively that they flip from
"never fire" to "fire far above prior rate", which distorts the rare-attribute
correlations in the opposite direction instead of fixing them. The initial-epoch
value jumping from 0.51 (iter 3 epoch 0) to 1.19 (iter 4 epoch 0) is consistent
with this: the very first validation pass with pos_weight already had
strongly-biased predictions. Secondarily, AdamW weight_decay=0.01 applied to
the pretrained ViT backbone likely pulled features toward zero faster than the
head could learn useful outputs. Because all three changes went in together,
it is impossible to separate blame, so the safest step is to roll them all back
and introduce just ONE targeted change this time.

Diagnosis for the remaining gap (iter 3's tail_mean = 0.22 > 0.15):
The iter-3 curve descends from 0.51 (epoch 0) to ~0.20 by epoch 20, then
oscillates in 0.20-0.25 for the final 10 epochs (std 0.067, min 0.199 at
epoch 29). This is a classic plateau-with-noise pattern: the fixed LR (5e-5)
is large enough that each epoch's gradient update keeps perturbing the
decision boundary around the 0.5 binarization threshold, causing individual
attributes to flip in/out of the "fires" state from epoch to epoch. Since
tail_mean is the average of the last 5 epochs, that oscillation is exactly
what keeps tail_mean stuck ~0.22 even though the best individual epochs
already touch 0.20.

A learning-rate decay that drops the LR to near-zero in the final epochs is
the textbook fix for this shape of curve. None of the prior iterations had
a scheduler in a configuration that kept the rest of the training dynamics
stable (iter 4 coupled cosine decay to pos_weight + weight_decay, which
swamped any isolated benefit).

Changes (one coherent intervention in lightningmodules/classification.py):

1. Add CosineAnnealingLR(T_max=max_epochs, eta_min=self.lr * 0.01), stepped
   per epoch. Keep Adam (NOT AdamW), keep zero weight_decay, keep unweighted
   BCEWithLogitsLoss. max_epochs is read from trainer.max_epochs with a
   fallback of 30 so the schedule lines up with Hparams.max_epochs=30.
   eta_min = lr/100 = 5e-7 — aggressive enough to actually settle the tail
   without being zero, and deeper than iter 4's lr*0.05 floor so the last
   5 epochs genuinely stop perturbing decisions.

   Why this helps the metric: in the tail epochs the LR is roughly
   5e-5 * cos(pi * 27/30)/2 + eta_min ≈ 3.8e-6 (epoch 27), 1.7e-6 (28),
   6.6e-7 (29). The updates are ~10-30x smaller than before, so per-attribute
   decisions near the 0.5 threshold stop flipping between epochs, reducing
   tail-epoch variance in P_pred. Mean should also improve: if epoch 29's
   0.199 is the "true" converged value, the tail mean should approach that
   instead of averaging over the noisy 0.20-0.25 band.
