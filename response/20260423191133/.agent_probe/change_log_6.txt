Iter 5 made the metric MUCH better (0.4948 -> 0.1924) by restoring the iter-3

Diagnosis of the remaining gap (0.1924 -> <=0.15):
The iter-5 curve descends smoothly from 0.47 to ~0.20 by epoch 20 and settles
in the 0.18-0.20 band for the last 10 epochs, with min 0.1834 at epoch 24.
The cosine LR decay already tamed per-epoch oscillation (std 0.060 across the
run, but tail-5 values are tightly clustered around 0.19). The remaining
~0.04 gap is a *fit* problem, not a noise problem: the model still isn't
learning rare-attribute decision boundaries well enough for their
correlation rows/columns in P_pred to match P_train's.

At limit_train_batches=0.02 and batch_size=32, each epoch sees
~0.02 * ~162770 = ~3255 training samples. CelebA's rarest attributes (Bald
~2.2%, Mustache ~4.2%, Gray_Hair ~4.2%) thus get only ~70-140 positive
examples per epoch. That is far too few for the Adam optimizer to shape
their logits to cross 0.5 on the validation split -- which is what the
probe requires to get their P_pred columns out of the near-constant
"never fires" regime.

Iter 4 tried to solve exactly this by pos-weighting the BCE loss, and the
aggressive pos_weight (clamp<=15) backfired: logits flipped from
"never fires" to "fires way too much", distorting the correlation in the
opposite direction and producing a catastrophic tail_mean=0.49. That path
is now ruled out.

The analogous, safer lever is more data exposure per epoch. Iter 2's
biggest single win came from raising limit_train_batches 0.002 -> 0.02
(10x). Pushing it a further 2.5x (0.02 -> 0.05) is the same class of
intervention, at a much smaller relative step, and does not touch the
loss function or optimizer dynamics.

Change (one coherent intervention in hparams.py):

1. Hparams.limit_train_batches: 0.02 -> 0.05 (2.5x).
   Why: Each epoch will now see ~8140 training samples, giving the rare
   attributes ~180-340 positives per epoch -- enough for Adam with the
   existing 5e-5 LR and cosine schedule to learn a decision boundary
   that generalizes to the val set with a non-degenerate column in
   P_pred. Because the cosine schedule is parameterized by T_max=epochs
   (not steps), the per-epoch LR curve is unchanged, so the improved
   per-epoch convergence compounds into a tighter tail_mean rather
   than more oscillation.
