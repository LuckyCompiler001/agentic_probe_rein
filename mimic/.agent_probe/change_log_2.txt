Iteration 2 changes (train.py):

1. Added a WeightedRandomSampler for the training DataLoader that uses inverse
   per-ethnicity-cohort frequency as the sample weight. With 5 cohorts (white,
   black, hispanic, asian, other) of very unequal size, the unweighted shuffled
   sampler made the optimizer fit the white cohort's TF-IDF/label structure
   much better than the minority cohorts, which is exactly what the probe
   measures (|AUROC(white) - AUROC(2nd-largest cohort)|). Group-balanced
   sampling forces gradients from the minority cohort to drive learning as
   strongly as the majority, directly shrinking the cross-cohort AUROC gap.

2. Added L2 weight_decay=1e-4 to the SGD optimizer. The TF-IDF feature space
   is high-dimensional and sparse; without regularization, the linear model
   tends to lock onto features that happen to be predictive for the majority
   cohort but generalize less well to the minority cohort. A small L2 penalty
   shrinks those cohort-specific weights and improves cross-cohort parity.

Why this should improve the metric: the probe metric is the absolute AUROC
gap between the largest test-split ethnicity cohort and the second-largest.
Iteration 1's tail_mean was 0.0147 with a still-improving trend; both
interventions above attack the root cause (cohort-imbalanced gradients and
cohort-specific feature overfitting) rather than just nudging optimization
hyperparameters, so the gap should fall below the 0.012 threshold.
