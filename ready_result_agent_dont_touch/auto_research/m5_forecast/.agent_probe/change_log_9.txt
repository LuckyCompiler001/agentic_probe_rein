Change: Raised EARLY_STOPPING_ROUNDS from 5 to 25 (single-line edit at the EARLY_STOPPING_ROUNDS constant). All other parameters were left untouched.

Why expected to lower validation RMSE: Looking at probe_result_7, validation RMSE was still slowly decreasing at epoch 85 (final value 1.8649, tail-mean 1.8648), with the curve trending downward at a small but non-zero rate. With EARLY_STOPPING_ROUNDS = 5, training halts on any 5-round stretch where the metric doesn't beat the running best — a tolerance much shorter than the natural epoch-to-epoch noise on this noisy retail-sales target. probe_result_8 demonstrated this exact failure mode by stopping 16 epochs earlier (69 vs 85) at a worse RMSE (1.8766 vs 1.8648) under the same code, purely because of stochastic timing of a noisy uptick. Raising tolerance to 25 (a 5x increase, the upper end of Regime B's 2x-5x moderate-step guidance, and roughly half the distance to the comment's named lower bound of 50) lets training continue through ordinary noise spikes up to the N_ESTIMATORS = 100 cap, capturing the additional gradual improvement the curve indicates is still available. This change has no interaction with other hyperparameters and carries low overshoot risk.
