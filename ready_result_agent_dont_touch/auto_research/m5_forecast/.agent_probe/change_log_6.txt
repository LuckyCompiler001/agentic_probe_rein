Why expected to lower validation RMSE: M5 daily unit sales are sparse non-negative counts dominated by zeros — a classic zero-inflated regression problem. Plain L2 ("regression") loss is symmetric and treats over- and under-predicting equally, which on heavily zero-skewed targets pulls means toward small positive values inefficiently. Tweedie loss models a compound Poisson-Gamma distribution and is the standard choice for this kind of intermittent retail-demand data; it typically reduces RMSE on M5 versus L2 even before tuning the variance power.
