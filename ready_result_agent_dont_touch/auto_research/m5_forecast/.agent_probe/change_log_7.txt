Chosen item: potential_improvement_8

Change: Raised NUM_LEAVES from 7 to 31 (single-line edit at the NUM_LEAVES constant). MAX_DEPTH and all other parameters were left untouched.

Why expected to lower validation RMSE: NUM_LEAVES = 7 severely caps the per-tree representational capacity of the booster, preventing it from carving out interactions among calendar (wday/month/year), price, lag_1, lag_7, and rolling_mean_28 features simultaneously. With ~22 active features and a deeply-stratified retail dataset (multiple stores × items × states × event/snap calendars), 7 leaves cannot encode the multivariate splits needed to differentiate per-series demand levels. Bumping to 31 (LightGBM's default) hits a moderate Regime-B refinement step (~5x the current value) without overshooting toward the comment's named upper end of 63-127, leaving room for further increases if the next probe shows clear improvement. Current tail_mean is 1.8801 — the model is clearly learning (down from a baseline-level RMSE of 3.24), so a moderate step is preferred over a full jump.
