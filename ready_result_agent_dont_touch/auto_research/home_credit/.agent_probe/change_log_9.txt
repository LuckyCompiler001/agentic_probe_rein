Change: Set FILLNA_VALUE from 0.0 to np.nan, so the
`merged.replace([inf,-inf], nan).fillna(FILLNA_VALUE)` step now leaves
missing values as NaN and lets LightGBM use its native missing-value
handling rather than collapsing NaN and 0 into the same numeric value.

Why this is expected to lift validation ROC-AUC: probe_result_7 plateaued
in the 0.7770-0.7773 band — the model is data-rich but currently sees
"missing" and "zero" as identical for hundreds of numeric columns
(application demographics, AMT_*, EXT_SOURCE_*, and the bureau / PREV /
POS / INSTAL / CC aggregates that produce many natural NaNs after the
left-join). LightGBM's native NaN handling learns a separate split
direction for missing values, recovering signal that 0-imputation
destroys. Treated as a categorical / discrete choice (0.0 vs np.nan)
under the always-fully-applied rule.
